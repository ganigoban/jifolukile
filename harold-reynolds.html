All you need to know about Harold Reynolds

Posted by Tandra Barner on Saturday, August 10, 2024

Harold Reynolds is an American baseball analyst and retired professional baseball player who has a net worth of $7 million.

Harold Reynolds was an All-Star and Gold Glove Award-winning second baseman in Major League Baseball, most notably with the Seattle Mariners, and he increased his net worth as an Emmy Award-w inning broadcast baseball analyst for MLB Network and Fox Sports.

Who is Harold Reynolds?

Harold Reynolds was born November 26, 1960, in Eugene, Oregon. He attended Corvallis High School, where he excelled in football, basketball, and baseball. Harold was a part of Corvallis’ American Legion baseball team, which won state and regional titles in 1978, as well as the state championship (AAA) football team.

He graduated in 1979 and was taken in the sixth round of the MLB Draft by the San Diego Padres, but he declined to sign. Instead, he attended San Diego State University before transferring to Canada College in Redwood City, CA.

How old is Harold Reynolds?

He is currently 63 years old.

What is Harold Reynolds’s net worth?

He is estimated to be worth $7 Million.

What is Harold Reynolds’s career?

The Seattle Mariners selected Harold Reynolds second overall in the first round of the amateur draft in 1980. He spent several years in their minor league system, where he learned to switch-hit. He made his major league debut on September 2, 1983, and bounced back and forth until his official rookie season in 1995.

Reynolds was an All-Star in 1987 and 1988, and he led the American League in stolen bases in 1987 with sixty. In addition, he led in triples with 11 in 1988 and at-bats with 642 in 1990. Harold won three Gold Glove Awards from 1988 to 1990 and led the American League in assists and double plays five times apiece.

Harold Reynolds got the Roberto Clemente Award in 1991. In the fall of 1992, he was allowed free agency and joined the Baltimore Orioles. He was with the team for one year before signing with the San Diego Padres. Reynolds’ final professional baseball season came in 1994, when he was traded to the California Angels in exchange for Hilly Hathaway.

Reynolds began working for ESPN in 1996 as a studio analyst for “Baseball Tonight.” Over the years, he has appeared at significant baseball events for the network, including the All-Star Game and World Series. He has also worked as a pundit for the College World Series and Little League World Series. In June 2007, he joined MLB.com as a pundit and collaborated with TBS on their coverage of Sunday baseball telecasts and the 2008 MLB playoffs.

Reynolds has been with the MLB Network since January 2009, appearing frequently on “MLB Tonight,” “Quick Pitch,” “Diamond Demo,” and breaking news and special event coverage. He was nominated for a Sports Emmy in 2011, 2012, and 2013. Reynolds joined the “MLB on Fox” show in 2012 and co-hosted the daily morning show “Hot Stove” with Matt Vasgersian during the offseason.
