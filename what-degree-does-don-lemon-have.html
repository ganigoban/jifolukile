What degree does Don Lemon have? – Don Lemon is an American journalist, author, and television news anchor who currently hosts CNN Tonight with Don Lemon.

Born on March 1, 1966, in Baton Rouge, Louisiana, he is the son of Katherine Clark and Ronald Lemon. Lemon grew up in a Catholic household and attended a Catholic school before transferring to a public high school.

Don Lemon received his undergraduate degree in broadcast journalism from Louisiana State University in Baton Rouge. During his college years, he worked at a local news station and interned at NBC in New York City. After graduation, he began his professional journalism career at WNYW, a Fox-affiliated station in New York City. He then worked for NBC News, including as a correspondent for NBC Nightly News, and for MSNBC.

In 2006, Don Lemon joined CNN, where he has since become a prominent figure in the network’s news programming. In addition to hosting CNN Tonight, he has also anchored CNN’s coverage of several major events, including the Boston Marathon bombing, the Sandy Hook Elementary School shooting, and the death of Nelson Mandela.

Throughout his career, Don Lemon has been recognized for his journalism work. He has won several awards, including an Edward R. Murrow Award and an Emmy Award, both for his coverage of the 2012 presidential election. Lemon has also been named to The Root 100 list of influential African Americans.

In addition to his work in journalism, Don Lemon has also written several books. His first book, Transparent, was published in 2011 and is a memoir about his life and career. In 2020, he published This is the Fire: What I Say to My Friends About Racism, which discusses the history of racism in the United States and his own experiences with racism.

Lemon has also been known for his candid discussions on race and social issues. He has been vocal about his own experiences with racism and has used his platform to address issues such as police brutality and systemic racism.

Lemon is openly gay and has been an advocate for LGBTQ+ rights. In 2011, he publicly came out as gay in his memoir Transparent. Since then, he has used his platform to promote acceptance and equality for the LGBTQ+ community.
