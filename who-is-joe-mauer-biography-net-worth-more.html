Who is Joe Mauer: Biography, Net Worth &amp; more

Posted by Martina Birk on Monday, August 26, 2024

Joe Mauer is a retired American professional baseball catcher and first baseman in the Major League Baseball (MLB). He played a total of 15 seasons in the league with the Minnesota Twins. Over the course of his career, he was named six time All-Star and holds the record as the only catcher in MLB history to win three batting titles, and the only catcher to ever win a batting title in the American League (AL).

Mauer played high school baseball, basketball and football for St. Paul’s Cretin-Derham Hall Raiders. During his senior year, he became the only athlete ever to be selected as the USA Today High School Player of the Year in two sports (football in 2000 as a quarterback and baseball in 2001 as a catcher).

Mauer won several accolades and honors, including 3x Gold Glove Award, 5x Silver Slugger Award, 3x AL Batting Champion, American League (AL) Most Valuable Player and Minnesota Twins Hall of Fame.

Who is Joe Mauer?

Joseph Patrick Mauer known simply as Joe Mauer was born on April 19, 1983 in Saint Paul, Minnesota, United States. He is the third of three children born to Teresa Mauer and Jake Mauer Jr. His older siblings are Jake Mauer and Billy Mauer.

Mauer attended St. Paul’s Cretin-Derham Hall Raiders, where he played baseball, basketball and football. He excelled in all the sports he played, particularly in football and baseball as he became the only the only athlete ever to be selected as the USA Today High School Player of the Year in two sports in his senior year.

During his high school baseball career, Mauer struck out only once during his four-year high school career, and hit .605 during his senior season. All through those years, his batting average was more than .500. He set a record and tied the national preps mark by hitting a home run in seven consecutive games.

After high school, Mauer decided to enter the Major League Baseball (MLB), although he initially had committed to play for the Florida State University. He was selected in the first overall pick of the 2001 MLB draft by the Minnesota Twins.

On April 5, 2004, Mauer made his MLB debut, where he finished the game 2-for-3 while hitting a single off Rafael Betancourt of the Cleveland Indians for his first major-league hit. He was sidelined for a month two days after making his debut, as he sustained an injury to his left medial meniscus, which required a surgery.

Mauer’s debut season did not go as planned as he was forced to prematurely end the season due to pain and swelling in his knee after he had undergone surgery and rehabilitation stints with the Twins’ Triple-A affiliate.

Mauer made a comeback in January 24, 2005 after he penned a contract for under a million with the Twins. In 2005, Mauer returned to the Twins’ lineup for his first full major league season, and batted .294 with 144 hits, nine home runs and 55 RBI in 131 games.
