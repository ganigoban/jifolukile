Hardeep Singh Nijjar Cause of Death

Hardeep Singh Nijjar, a Khalistani terrorist who was wanted by the Indian government, was shot and killed in Surrey, Canada. Nijjar served as the chief of the Khalistan Tiger Force (KTF) and was one of 40 designated terrorists on the Indian government’s list.

According to reports, Nijjar’s body was found with bullet wounds inside a car parked at the Guru Nanak Sikh Gurdwara Sahib in Surrey, where he served as the head. The incident occurred at 8:27 pm (local time) on Sunday, and officials believe that Nijjar was shot by two unidentified attackers and died on the spot.

Following the shooting, Canadian police moved Nijjar’s body from the scene, during which a group of Sikhs reportedly shouted pro-Khalistan and anti-India slogans.

Biography of Hardeep Singh Nijjar

Hardeep Singh Nijjar was known for leading a campaign and spreading propaganda against India. He organized protests outside of Indian missions in several countries such as the US, UK, Germany, and Canada. He also allegedly ordered targeted killings in Punjab.

Nijjar worked alongside other designated terrorists such as Gurpatwant Singh Pannun and Paramjit Singh Pamma under Sikhs for Justice (SFJ), which was banned by the Ministry of Home Affairs in 2019.

The National Investigation Agency listed his residences in Canada as 8193, 143-A Street, Surrey BC, Canada and 1418, 142 Street, 72 Avenue, BC (British Columbia) Canada. The SFJ was described as a front organization of Khalistan terrorist outfits operating from foreign soils including Pakistan.

Furthermore, Nijjar forcibly took over Surrey’s Guru Nanak Sikh temple to become its president. In recent years, he frequently participated in protests in front of the Consulate General of India in Vancouver.
